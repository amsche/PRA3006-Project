How does it work?

1. The Website

When loading the page, it sends a SPARQL Query to WikiData and retrieves all infectious diseases represented in the database. The names of these infectious diseases are stored in the dropdown menu of the search. This ensures that only the infectious diseases included in WikiData are available in the search.


2. The Disease

An infectious disease can be searched by typing letters into the searchbar. All infectious diseases including these letters will subsequently be displayed in the dropdown menu. From the dropdown menu, one infectious disease can be chosen by clicking on it. This action also sends a second SPARQL Query to WikiData in order to retrieve all symptoms and their descriptions associated with the selected disease.


3. The Symptoms

All symptoms linked to the selected infectious disease are displayed in the shape of a circle. When clicking on a specific symptom, the circle will rotate until the symptom is displayed on the right side of the circle. A discription of the symptom is given in the speech bubble to the right of the circle.


4. The Drugs

Next to the discription of the chosen symptom, a button with the option to select the symptom can be found. When a symptom is selected, a third SPARQL Query is send to WikiData in order to retrieve the drugs that cure the chosen symptom. These drugs are displayed in a circle underneath the circle with the symptoms. When a second symptom is selected, the drugs to cure it are similarly retrieved from WikiData, and displayed in a second circle. Drugs that cure both the first and the second selected symptom are shown in the overlap between the two circles (Venn Diagram) A third symptom can be selected and added to the Venn Diagram.
